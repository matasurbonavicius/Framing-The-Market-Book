## The Book

This book was a 6-months project to dive as deep as I could into what constitutes a trading plan and also - how to actually plan the results of the trading "system" prior to even testing the system, which is most often the case with discretionary trading.

This was analyzed through two lenses:

1. What is a trading plan? -> essentially a documentation of a lifecycle of a single trade. Then by repeating that lifecycle -> contratulations you are trading
2. How to actually plan the results without even testing the system? -> look into position sizing strategy and the desired risk/return ratio, which determines the win rate. With these, one can simulate the distrubution of possible outcomes over a given period of trades and calculate probabilities.

What I aimed to find was a way to write a trading plan that will make me profitable long term.

What I found is a deeper desire to build algorithmic trading systems, rather than trade discretionary.

1. If you want to trade with a trading plan, then sure, do it. But you can write a trading plan as a set of rules around the trade lifecycle (how do you want to make trades). And if you can write a set of rules, then you can automate it. Thats quant.

2. If you truly want to be a discretionary trader, then a trading plan is not the way to go. You have to know the markets by the gut, have courage and discipline, go out there and absolutely dominate. But this is not something that any trader can put onto paper.
